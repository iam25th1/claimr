Swap card - now actually useful

What you saw: 10 USDC -> 0.39 EURC. That's the Starlight pool's actual
math. The pool was seeded with way more USDC than EURC, so the constant-
product formula gives a brutal rate when you swap into the under-supplied
side. We can't fix the pool. We CAN make sure you see what's coming
before you swap.

What this batch does

  components/claimr/swap-card.tsx           REBUILT

  Reads the pool's actual reserves of USDC and EURC by calling
  balanceOf(STARLIGHT_POOL_ADDRESS) on each token contract. Refetches
  every 10 seconds.

  Computes a live quote using the standard x*y=k AMM formula:
      amountOut = (amountIn * reserveOut) / (reserveIn + amountIn)
  before you swap. So if you type 10 USDC, you see "0.39 EURC" before
  hitting the button. No surprise.

  Shows a rate-quality badge based on the implied slippage from a 1:1
  peg (both are ~$1 stables):
      >= 90% efficient   green checkmark, "Good rate"
      >= 50% efficient   yellow info, "X% slippage from 1:1"
      <  50% efficient   red warning, "Pool would take X% of your value"

  Shows the actual pool reserves below the quote so you understand why
  the rate is what it is:
      Pool reserves:  500.00 USDC / 19.50 EURC

  When the rate is poor, shows a banner suggesting a smaller amount to
  try (1% of the destination reserve, where slippage is much lower).

  Keeps the "You received X.XXXX EURC" feedback after the swap completes.


How this changes the demo

  Before: user types 10 USDC, sees no estimate or a fake 0.918 estimate,
          swaps, gets burned, feels deceived.

  After:  user types 10 USDC, immediately sees "you'll get ~0.39 EURC,
          which is a 96% haircut" and a banner saying "try 0.20 USDC
          instead, where pool depth makes the rate much better."


Why this can't be fully fixed in Claimr

  The Starlight pool contract on Arc Testnet
  (0xB70CBc5F65B2CEC9585cF061ac6b5CD9c462CE53) has imbalanced reserves.
  Each USDC into the pool can only buy a fraction of an EURC because
  there's very little EURC to give out. This is an on-chain liquidity
  problem at the pool, not a UI problem. If you ever own / control the
  pool or know who does, balancing the reserves is the long-term fix.

  Until then, this UI makes the rate visible before you commit, which
  is the right hackathon move.
